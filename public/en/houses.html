Two Houses, One Parliament

House of Representatives & National Assembly

Nepal's Federal Parliament is bicameral: a lower house, the House of Representatives, and an upper house, the National Assembly. See each house's role, composition, and official sources below.

House of Representatives

Lower House · Directly elected

The House of Representatives is the lower house of the Federal Parliament, with 275 members total: 165 elected directly (first-past-the-post) and 110 elected by proportional representation. It serves a 5-year term and is presided over by the Speaker. Government formation and confidence votes are decided here.

National Assembly

Upper House · Permanent house

The National Assembly is the upper, permanent house of the Federal Parliament, with 59 members total: 56 elected by an electoral college (8 per province) and 3 nominated by the President. Members serve 6-year terms, with one-third retiring every 2 years, so the house is never fully dissolved. The Chairperson presides.
